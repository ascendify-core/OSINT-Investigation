# **1\. What We Wanted to Find Out**

Our question (called a PIR - Priority Intelligence Requirement) was simple: who runs OpenAI, how is it structured, how is it funded, what does its online presence look like, and is it involved in any lawsuits? Everything below comes from public websites, news, and OpenAI's own official pages - nothing private or hacked.

# **2\. Who Owns and Runs OpenAI**

OpenAI has two main parts working together:

- OpenAI Foundation - a nonprofit. It doesn't make money itself, but it controls the company and picks the board.
- OpenAI Group PBC - the for-profit business side. This is where products, revenue, and daily operations happen.
- Microsoft - a major investor. It owns a large chunk of the company but doesn't have a board seat.

_In plain terms: Think of the Foundation as the "guardian" that makes sure the company sticks to its mission, while the PBC is the actual business making and selling products._

_Source: openai.com/our-structure_

_In plain terms: Raising this much money, this fast, usually means the company is spending an enormous amount - most likely on the computer hardware needed to build AI models. It also suggests they're preparing to become a publicly traded company._

_Source: Wikipedia - OpenAI_

# **4\. OpenAI's Online Presence (GitHub)**

OpenAI has an official GitHub page - a website where companies share their code publicly. It has around 261 projects and 125,000+ followers, which is a lot.

- openai-cookbook - example code to help developers use OpenAI's tools
- whisper - a free speech-to-text tool OpenAI released publicly
- tiktoken - a small but widely used tool many other companies now depend on

_In plain terms: A company sharing this much free code is a way of getting developers hooked on its tools - it's a business strategy, not just generosity._

_Source: github.com/openai_

# **5\. Legal Trouble**

OpenAI's biggest ongoing legal issue is a group of lawsuits (combined into one big case) from authors, publishers, and news companies. They claim OpenAI used their copyrighted books and articles to train its AI without permission.

| **When** | **What happened**                                                                              |
| -------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Nov 2025 | Court made OpenAI hand over internal messages about deleted training data                      |
| Jan 2026 | Court ordered OpenAI to hand over 20 million ChatGPT conversations, despite privacy objections |
| Mar 2026 | Court ordered even more conversation logs to be handed over                                    |
| May 2026 | A separate case (Elon Musk vs. OpenAI) ended with a jury ruling in OpenAI's favor              |

_In plain terms: The important takeaway here isn't the lawsuit itself - it's that courts have ruled ChatGPT conversations can be legally handed over during a lawsuit. That means conversations aren't as private as many users might assume once legal proceedings are involved._

_Source: Bloomberg Law, Jan 5 2026; Wikipedia - OpenAI_

# **6\. What We Can Conclude**

- OpenAI is shifting from a nonprofit-controlled mission toward a normal, investor-driven company heading for a stock market listing.
- Its spending is enormous, most likely on computing power - that's probably the main thing limiting how fast it can grow.
- Its biggest legal risk is the copyright lawsuit, and it's already lost some privacy arguments about user data during that case.

How sure are we? Very confident about the structure and money facts (they come straight from OpenAI or very reliable news). Less confident about how the lawsuits will end - that's still playing out.
